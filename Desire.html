Like a burning ember kept at bay by the wind. Each time it weakens there is a great sense of relief. Without the wind, it grows larger, and at a certain point it is considered a fire. This is a metaphor for desire, gratification, and suffering.

Two goals: sustainability and liberation. All gratification should be conducive to these two.

  • Potential: capacity for gratification.
  • Purity: undesirable side effects are negative, neutral is zero, and desirable side effects are positive. Effects of both objects of desire, as well as desire itself. Effects conducive to sustainability and liberation.

1. Desire Analogy

taNhA is like desire. It compels one to act. This desire can be generated, restrained, or attenuated by satisfaction, with the goal of maintaining it below a threshold in short and long term duration.

2. Thirst Quenching

Enjoyment is a dynamic sequence of thirst quenching. When one continuously takes pleasure in an object, he is constantly thirsting and constantly quenching. So as a result, there is dukkha, but it is quite minimal so long as the thirst is being quenched.

3. Thirst Suppression?

For example, sleeping while drowsy, or calming the mind with music, the mind rests in consciousnesness of those obejcts. But is there any thirst during that period? I am not able to determine.

4. Focused Streams of Indulgence

The mind can settle its desires onto a category of renewable objects, and remain continuously satisfied to various degrees until the objects run out, the mind becomes satiated, or when undesirable side effects accumulate past a threshold.

For example, one can indulge in a bag of chips while he is hungry, and while doing so, all extraneous desires become suppressed as the mind revels in the various desirable objects of those chips. But as the conditions become exhausted by way of satiation, and the undesirable side effects in the form of bodily feelings build up, that stream of indulges eventually loses viability.

5. Dealing with the Undesired

  • Ignorance
  • Suppression through ignorance caused by indulgence in a stream

6. Curating the Desires

Allow only those desires which can be satisfied to arise, prevent or transmute the unresolvable.

7. Purity of Indulgence

Gratification can have undesirable side effects. Minimize this, and make use of other effects where they can’t be eliminated.

8. A Favorable Solution

The existing reality upgraded into the tight streams, retaining backwards compatibility. Wisdom and memories are also retained.

9. Extinction

  • Extinction
  • Destined for extinction
  • Knowledge of the path to extinction
  • Capacity for gaining that knowledge

  • Resources for finding and attaining it

    Know and attain extinction.

10. Attenuation

Possessing sustainable potential for immediate or pleasurable attenuation of any magnitude.

11. Productive Purity

Having only those desires which are clean and easy to satisfy, and are productive to sustaining purity and attenuative potential, and especially achieving extinction.

12. Types of Desire

Bright desires are those which are fulfilled through actions with bright results, and likewise for dark and neutral desires. Most desire is mixed, it is hard to recall a desire that is completely pure. Perhaps the sublime abidings?

Easy desires are easy and imminent to fulfill, and likewise for difficult desires.

The goal, then, is to cultivate desires that are as bright and easy as possible, and to withstand and let go of those desires that are dark and difficult.

13. The Dark Desire Problem

Dark desires are fulfilled through action with dark results, and thus bring suffering in the future in the form of more desires that are dark and difficult to fulfill. And what if these dark desires are long lasting, and difficult to withstand? What can be done to prevent those dark actions from taking place? Its either you or him, how will you withstand the suffering, and will it ever end?

Without fulfillment, dissolve it or suppress it with ignorance or distraction with bright desires.

Without fulfillment, dilute it with bright actions and fulfill as little of it as possible.
