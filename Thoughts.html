2. Abstract

Seeking epistemically certain solutions to the fundamental problem of existence.

Herein all statements are made from the subjective standpoint.

  • Dissatisfaction is the fundamental problem of existence.
  • The strength of the desire and the amount of the disparity between the desired and the attained determines its magnitude.
  • A high standard of satisfaction can be maintained through the minimization of these factors.
  • Cessation of desire is the ultimate solution.

Although it may be hard to see at times, desire is a volitional action. Even in dreams, desire is volitional. When one desires that which is readily available, he is satisfied. But when it is not, dissatisfaction arises. And the volitions could cause changes in experience and/or the tendency of the mind to desire this or that in the future.

So forget about the volition for now. Lets talk choices. At the end of the day, the choices are the only thing you have direct control over. Therefore, to gain and maintain satisfaction above a threshold, you should make all the choices that are condusive to that goal. Which choices do you make?

Assuming perfect knowlege, one would make all the ideal choices that would lead to the best possible outcome. However, I don’t claim perfect knowledge and am not sure I would ever be able to do so. Therefore, I’ve got to take my shots anyhow.

Two grades of choices: deliberate and habitual. Habitual choices aren’t necessarily bad; given the limitations of the body and mind, habituation is innevitable. And since there seems to be a great stablility to the mechanics of exietence thus far, aligining my habits to those mechanics proves to be very fruitful. So, through deliberate action establish favorable habits and maintain them. Then you act in line both deliberately and habitually.

Additionally, make choices that lead to greateer epistemic certainty through knowledge and upgrade your habits accordingly. See the similie of the man who carried hemp; kaizen, continuous improvement by way of refining one’s knowledge.

So essentially there are two relevant elements here:

  1. Knowledge
  2. Action

Knowledge existing for the sole purpose of guiding the action.

3. Abstract

The fundamental problem of existence is discontentment. It arises due to craving, is quelled by gratification, and eradicated through extinction.

Discontentment is typically experienced in association with the range of unpleaseant phenomena. From the subtlest of boredom to the most abject of suffering, discontentment is present in all.

The solution is arranged into a framework of volitional action. The goal is to constrain volitional action along an ideal sequence which results in the maximization of contentment in a particular fashion.

A pure mind does not crave or cling, it remains completely unaffected.

Just as thirst is attenuated through drink, so is craving in the mind attenuated through qualia.

But what is the cause of craving? What will it crave and why? Is there an unconditional end to the cause of craving? Just as a fire is forever extinguished when doused in water, is it also the case when the cause of ignorance is drenched in the light of knowledge? Does this knowledge persist even after it has served its function? Need it necessarily persist to maintain this state?

When a desirable object is apprehended, craving is intensified and the mind clings to it. The apprehension of the craving seems to presuppose this craving. Why does it occur this way? Is this response invariable to time? Is there some thing within the mind which causes it to respond this way?

What is discontentment? It is personal objection. Whenever there is an objection, there is discontentment. Whenever there is a craving, there is discontentment, and once that craving is satisfied, there is contentment.

This discontentment manifests itself as inclination, boredom, interest, anticipation, wishfullness, craving, longing, dissatisfaction, hunger, drive, …, suffering, despair, & torture according to its intensity.

Now, the human solution is quite simple: become a monk and reside in the sublime abodes. Amazing. But the real focus is beyond this life…

So, the problem exists and it is to be minimized and eradicated. With the input of action, what what one do at every moment in pursuit of this goal?

10. Intro Essay

From time immemorial mankind has pursued a single goal: the satisfaction of desire. It is a goal that is existant and evident in every human experience, one that transcends all boundries of language and culture. We all wish to be free of boredom, pain, and misery. We all wish to be well and happy, this is the single universal principle which unites all known conscious entites.

And with the frustration of desire comes dissatisfaction, manifesting itself in many forms. From the subtlest boredeom to the most abject of suffering, dissatisfaction is present in them all. Seeking to resolve this dissatisfaction, mankind has shaped and transformed the world in many ways, building entire civilisations of high technology in pursuit of greater satisfaction.

But even with all this magical technology, we are still subject to this very same element of dissatisfaction that our ancestors faced many hundreds of millenia in the past. Not even the wealthiest among men, replete with the potential of catering to their every desire, is free from this common experience of boredom, pain, sickness, old age, and ultimately death.

And with the innevitable destruction of the body comes the uncertainty of the afterlife. Does one continue to exist beyond death? Will we continue to experience this same dissatisfaction once again, at a lesser or greater extent?

10.1. The solution

Though enshrined in complexity, this problem of dissatisfaction can be broken down into a simple and self evident model of desire and its gratification. At the root of dissatisfaction is a craving, an objection to the current state of things and a yearning for another. When we get thirsty, we crave hydration. And when we obtain that drink, we become satisfied. This simple yet fundamental mechanic is present in every instance of subjective existence, from the subtlest notes of intrest all the way up to the heights of misery. Satisfaction is the gratification of desire.

So the solution is quite simple: keep the mind satisfied to a high degree by empowering it with a swift potential for gratification, and maintain this state all the while remaining just as satisfied. And even better, minimize or even eradicate the desires themselves so that the very possibility of dissatisfaction becomess severed at its root. Indefinite form of happiness.

The desirability of phenomenon seems to be influenced by a stable underlying factor.

We seek to indefinitely minimize this dissatisfaction below a standard - perpetual happiness - through epistemic certainty.

11. The Problem

Dissatisfaction is the fundamental problem of existence. It is the only problem I can see now (at the time of writing), the only problem I can remember in the past, and the only problem I can forsee in the future. It is solved temporarily by satisfaction, and permanantly by eradication.

Considering a unit of existence, there is desire, and there are the objects of awareness. Dissatisfaction is the disparity between the desired state of those objects and the actual. The goal is to minimize and maintain that disparity below a standard.

Each instance of desire and awareness of objects could influence the form of both in the future, so an optimal causal sequence is sought. There are also external factors, whether or not an induvidual can control these is uncertain.

25. A Similie

Imagine a sphere floating in space, being moved around by a person’s arm. He has semi-controllable spasms which drive him to move around the ball. The movement of the ball produces pleasurable feelings proportional to its speed, but the ball can also resist movement. Here, the force of the arm is thirst, movement and pleasure is its satiation, and the resistance of the ball causes sustained unresolved thirst which of course is stress. So the arm, being the impure mind, flails around and generates pleasure, and experiences stress when there is resistance.

53. Theory of Desire

What is the source of desire? When there is an apparent desire, consciousness of its corresponding object brings about its gratification. When the desire is not apparent, still, consciousness of the same object can still bring about gratification, as if the desire was latent and only made active upon contact with its object.

As desire is being continually gratified, there is a great resolution and attenuation of desires that would otherwise be present. How does this all work? By understanding the machanics of desire, an ideal being and world can be constructed such that there is a continual gratification of a high degree.

54. Slices of the Ultimate

66.5. Subjectivity

All problems are subjective. There is not a single phenomenon which is inherently problematic. Here are some qualia that some may consider problematic, while others may not:

  • The taste of certain foods, like meat, fish, coffee, durian, etc.
  • Bitterness in Food: Some find bitterness, like that in coffee or dark chocolate, unpleasant, while others enjoy it for its complexity.
  • ASMR Sounds: Soft whispering or tapping can relax some people, but can also annoy or even disturb others who experience “misophonia.”
  • Bright or Neon Colors: Bright, bold colors can seem joyful and invigorating to some, but harsh or overstimulating to others.
  • Heat and Humidity: Warm, humid environments feel comforting to some, while others find them stifling and uncomfortable.
  • Silence or Stillness: Silence may evoke peace for some, while for others, it leads to discomfort or even anxiety.
  • Strong Scents: Strong fragrances like perfumes, flowers, or certain foods (like durian or garlic) can delight some but repel others due to their intensity.
  • Intense or Dissonant Music: Some forms of music with complex or dissonant harmonies can be deeply moving for some listeners but jarring or unsettling for others.

In brief, the problematic aspect of qualia is distinct from the qualia itself.

Given that all problems are subjective in nature, we idintify its origin within consciousness itself, in the form of thirst, or craving.

When a person is thirsty, there is a persistent push towards hydration, and as a result that person acts in accordance until that thirst is finally quenched. Likewise in the scenario where a person craves a certain food.

This craving is the source of both desire and aversion, both being manifestations thereof.

It is fundamental, as without this craving there cannot be any objection.
